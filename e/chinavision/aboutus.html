About Us

China Vision

Since 1999 China Vision has been working to enhance educational opportunities of visually impaired and other disabled people in China. We believe that equal access to education is a prerequisite to building social justice and equality.

Education in its broadest sense implies open, unfettered access to information and freedom of thought and discussion. China Vision has supported both individuals and organisations of disabled people to build information platforms, connect with disabled people across China, and engage in the international discourse on disability rights.

Our projects provide professional capacity-building and support for disabled people’s groups, helping them build radio and internet services, providing a voice to the voiceless, giving access to essential information and connecting communities. China Vision is helping disabled people to discover creative solutions to the problems that affect us all.

Who we are

ChinaVision is a UK-based charity, set up in 1999 by a group of individuals concerned about the lives and development of blind and partially-sighted people in China. Our trustees and members include specialists in Chinese affairs, disability, education and the broadcasting media. China Vision is a UK-registered charity.

Our Mission

China Vision supports individuals and organisations working to empower and enhance the rights of people with disabilities in China. We form people-to-people links, sharing experiences to drive practical actions aimed at improving the lives of people with visual and other disabilities. By sharing information and resources internationally we work to reduce discrimination and enhance social equality.

We Value:

• Human dignity - which includes respect, equality and empowerment
• Diversity - which enriches all human experience;
• Authenticity - which comes from people speaking for themselves
• Our disabilities - which are our greatest gifts

What we do

Since we set up China Vision in 1999 we have:
• Facilitated UK scholarships for visually impaired teachers from Inner Mongolia and Shandong
• Provided Braille and other English language reading materials to blind individuals and schools in China
• Sent “use it again” equipment from the UK to visually impaired people in China
• Supported the development of a comprehensive radio service for visually impaired people in China
• Support and monitored ‘Mobile Advice Clinics’ run for disabled people in the Chinese countryside
• Supported an online magazine containing Chinese translations of articles on disability from across the world
• Facilitated training for visually impaired photographers in Beijing
